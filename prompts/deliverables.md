# Deliverable Generation Prompts

Turn data analysis into stakeholder-ready artifacts — presentations, reports, and summaries.

## Presentations

> Make me a presentation about the relationship between car crashes and pavement distress on Blue Hill Avenue. Include crash statistics from Vision Zero, pavement condition data from Cyvl, street-level imagery showing the damage, and a recommendation for prioritized repair.

> Build a presentation on sidewalk conditions in Dorchester. Use imagery search to find cracked sidewalks, combine with 311 complaints, and recommend priority repair areas.

> Create a presentation comparing construction activity across Boston neighborhoods. Include active work zone data, imagery of construction sites, and a summary of where investment is concentrated.

## Reports

> Write a report on the top 5 most dangerous streets in Boston. For each street, include crash count, crash types (motor vehicle, pedestrian, bike), pavement condition score, and a street-level photo.

> Generate a curb management report for Downtown Boston — loading zones, bike lanes, ADA ramps, and any conflicts between uses.

## Quick Summaries

> Give me a one-page summary of infrastructure conditions around City Hall. Include pavement scores, active construction, recent 311 complaints, and imagery of any issues found.

> Summarize the current state of Blue Hill Avenue — crashes, pavement, construction, complaints — in a format I can share with the mayor's office.

## Tips

- Presentations work best when you specify what sections to include
- Always request street-level imagery — photos are more persuasive than numbers for non-technical stakeholders
- Ask for recommendations, not just data — "prioritized repair list" > "list of scores"
- For budget meetings, ask for cost/impact framing: "which repairs would prevent the most crashes?"
