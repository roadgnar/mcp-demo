# Boston Infrastructure Prompts

Neighborhood-level prompts for Boston's crash corridors, sidewalks, construction, and cross-MCP analysis.

## Crash Corridors

> Analyze crash patterns on Blue Hill Ave -- how many crashes per year, what modes (pedestrian, bike, motor vehicle), and where do they cluster?

> Compare crash frequency on Washington Street vs Mass Ave. Which corridor is more dangerous for pedestrians?

> Show me fatal crashes in Boston. Are they concentrated on specific streets?

## Sidewalk Conditions

> Search for cracked sidewalks in Dorchester -- show me 3 images and map the locations.

> Find damaged curb ramps in Roxbury. Are any near schools or transit stops?

> Compare sidewalk conditions in Back Bay vs Mattapan using imagery search.

## Construction and Work Zones

> What active work zones are there in the South End right now? Show me the streets and project categories.

> Are there crashes near current construction zones? Cross-reference work zones with Vision Zero data.

> Find construction equipment in street-level imagery near Downtown and compare to the official work zone list.

## Cross-MCP Analysis

> Do crashes on Blue Hill Ave correlate with pavement conditions? Pull crash data from Vision Zero and pavement scores from Cyvl.

> Compare 311 pothole complaints in Dorchester to actual pavement distress from imagery. Where are the gaps?

> What's the relationship between sidewalk damage (imagery) and 311 sidewalk complaints in Roxbury? Are residents reporting what's actually there?

## Reports and Deliverables

> Create a Washington Street infrastructure assessment for the city council -- pavement, crashes, 311, work zones, and photos.

> Build a Dorchester sidewalk conditions brief with imagery evidence and 311 complaint trends.

> Generate a crash corridor comparison report: Blue Hill Ave vs Washington St vs Mass Ave, with recommendations.
